Our Story

Building a Stronger, More Loving Community

The Madison Good Neighbor Gathering is an annual free outdoor festival on Madison's near west side that brings together hundreds of families to celebrate the diverse cultures and vibrant spirit of our neighborhoods.

What started as a simple idea to connect neighbors has grown into a beloved tradition — live entertainment, hands-on kids' activities, local food, and community resources, all in one beautiful park on the shores of Monona Bay.

Organized by neighbors, for neighbors. This event is a love letter to Madison's District 13 community.

Got Questions?

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything families usually want to know before heading out.

Is the Good Neighbor Gathering really free?
Yes, 100% free! There are no tickets to buy, no registration to fill out, and no hidden fees. Just show up with your family and have fun. The entire event — activities, entertainment, and community resources — is completely free of charge.
What time does the event run?
The Madison Good Neighbor Gathering runs from 12:00 PM to 4:00 PM on Sunday, September 13, 2026. Arrive anytime during those hours — there's no set schedule you have to follow.
What if it rains?
The Good Neighbor Gathering is a rain-or-shine event! Pack a rain jacket just in case, and come enjoy the fun regardless of the weather. The event is only cancelled in the case of severe weather — check our Instagram for any updates.
Is there parking nearby?
Yes! Free street parking is available on the streets surrounding Brittingham Park. Parking can fill up on nice days, so arriving a bit early or taking the bus or biking is a great option. Metro Transit has several routes nearby, and BCycle docking stations are close to the park.
Is the event stroller and wheelchair accessible?
Absolutely! Brittingham Park has paved paths throughout, making it easy to navigate with strollers, wheelchairs, or other mobility devices. All event activities are set up with accessibility in mind.
Can I bring my dog?
Yes! Dogs on a leash are welcome at Brittingham Park. Just be sure to clean up after your pup and keep them comfortable in the crowd.
Is there food available?
Yes! Multiple food carts will be on-site offering a variety of family-friendly and diverse food options. Food vendors accept both cash and card — no ATM on site, so plan ahead. You're also welcome to bring your own picnic — just grab a blanket and find a spot on the lawn!
How can I volunteer or sponsor the event?
We'd love your support! Email taylor@neighborhoodhouse.org to learn about volunteer opportunities or sponsorship packages. Your support helps keep this community event free and growing each year.
